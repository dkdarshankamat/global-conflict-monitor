Yemen – Saudi Arabia Conflict (2025–2040)

Estimated Deaths

From 2025 to 2040, over 380,000 people died as a result of continuing drone strikes, naval blockades, humanitarian crises, and internal clashes among militias, despite global efforts to end the war.

Historical Peace Efforts

  • 2025: The Geneva II Yemen Peace Accord collapsed after Houthi factions rejected oil-sharing terms.
  • 2029: Iran and Saudi Arabia established a joint "Observation Corridor" in Marib with limited UN presence.
  • 2033: The African Union facilitated talks in Djibouti—initially promising but later derailed due to cross-border drone attacks.
  • 2038: The “Green Crescent Pact” signed by southern Yemeni tribes offered local ceasefires, but lacked enforcement capacity.

Energy & Tech Impacts

Red Sea shipping routes were disrupted frequently due to drone raids and missile threats. Saudi Arabia developed high-altitude counter-drone towers, while Yemen saw a rise in decentralized solar-powered militia tech. Global oil prices spiked during the 2032 Bab el-Mandeb crisis.

Current Status Quo (2040)

Yemen remains fractured: Northern Yemen is Houthi-controlled, the South operates under a semi-autonomous alliance supported by the UAE, and the central government is exiled. Saudi Arabia maintains a fortified southern border and no-fly zones. A fragile ceasefire has held since late 2039, but trust remains low.
