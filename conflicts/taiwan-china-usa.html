Taiwan – China – USA Conflict (1945–Present)

Estimated Deaths

Between 2025 and 2040, escalating tensions and near-military confrontations led to 60,000+ deaths, primarily due to cyber-attacks, naval skirmishes in the Taiwan Strait, and regional missile exchanges during the 2034 crisis.

Historical Peace Efforts

  • 1992: “1992 Consensus” (informal agreement) where both sides acknowledged “One China” with different interpretations.
  • 1993: Koo–Wang Talks marked first official cross-strait dialogue.
  • 2005: Pan-Blue visit to China led to symbolic easing of party-to-party tensions.
  • 2008–2016: Cross-Strait Agreements under Taiwan's Ma Ying-jeou included direct flights, trade, and tourism deals.
  • 2015: Historic Xi–Ma Summit in Singapore marked first meeting of leaders since 1949.
  • 2026: The “Digital Firewall Agreement” attempted to curb state-sponsored cyber sabotage — partially successful but poorly enforced.
  • 2029: UN-backed talks in Singapore stalled after Taiwan reaffirmed sovereignty.
  • 2034: U.S. destroyer incident near the Kinmen Islands led to a weeklong naval confrontation; de-escalated through backchannel diplomacy in Tokyo.
  • 2038: ASEAN-led mediation created a “No-Drone” buffer zone over Taiwan Strait.

Energy & Tech Impacts

Global semiconductor supply chains were severely disrupted from 2030 to 2035. Taiwan fast-tracked offshore AI manufacturing facilities, while China invested in quantum-resistant cyber defenses. The U.S. established “Pacific Tech Corridors” to reduce dependence on East Asian chip routes.

Current Status Quo

Taiwan remains de facto independent with limited international recognition. China maintains heavy military presence along its southeastern coast. The U.S. Seventh Fleet operates under expanded joint-defense protocols with Japan and Taiwan. While open war has been avoided, both sides remain locked in a high-tech cold war.
