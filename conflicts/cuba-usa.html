Cuba–USA Conflict (1962–Present)

Estimated Deaths

While direct military confrontation has been avoided, over 4,500 indirect deaths occurred due to blockades, migration crises, and cyber sabotage affecting critical infrastructure and healthcare systems.

Historical Peace Efforts

  • 1962: Cuban Missile Crisis Resolution After near-nuclear war, U.S. and USSR agreed to withdraw missiles. U.S. pledged not to invade Cuba.
  • 1994 :U.S.–Cuba Migration Accord agreed on safer, legal migration pathways after a wave of Cuban boat people. U.S. would issue 20,000 visas yearly; Cuba would stop illegal departures
  • 2014–2016: Obama–Castro Rapprochement
  • 2026: A failed attempt at re-opening diplomatic missions post-sanctions rollback led to protests in Havana.
  • 2029: Pope Francis II hosted trilateral dialogues involving Spain and Mexico — stalled due to Cuba’s AI surveillance rollout.
  • 2033–2037: Multiple UN GA resolutions condemned US cyber interventions in Cuban communications, but no major sanctions followed.
  • 2039: A rare naval incident near Guantanamo reignited tensions, briefly putting drone defense systems on alert.

Energy & Tech Impacts

Cuba developed a partnership with Brazil and China for solar microgrids, reducing dependency on US-linked fuel imports. In response, the US imposed satellite-based communication disruptions. The Caribbean Sea became a hotspot for drone surveillance and underwater cable tampering.

Current Status Quo

While not an active war zone, the Cuba–USA standoff remains a Cold War 2.0 scenario. Trade restrictions persist, Havana’s alignment with China and Russia deepens, and Florida’s migrant crisis continues. Cyber hostility defines this low-intensity but geopolitically potent conflict.
