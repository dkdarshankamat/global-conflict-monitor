Sudan–South Sudan Conflict (2025–2040)

Estimated Deaths

Between 2025 and 2040, an estimated 215,000 lives have been lost due to sporadic border skirmishes, internal rebellions in South Kordofan and Blue Nile, and resource-driven conflicts in Unity State and Abyei.

Historical Peace Efforts

  • 2025: The Juba Talks resumed but collapsed after oil-sharing disputes.
  • 2028–2030: AU-led demilitarized buffer zones reduced direct combat by 60%.
  • 2035: A joint energy corridor was proposed but sabotaged by rebel factions.
  • 2039: A tentative peace accord signed with Chinese mediation remains fragile.

Energy & Tech Impacts

South Sudan’s oil fields saw increased Chinese and Indian investment. Pipelines through Ethiopia faced repeated cyberattacks. Drone surveillance by Turkey, and the deployment of Russian-built energy microstations in contested regions, shaped the modern battlefield.

Current Status Quo (2040)

The conflict remains unresolved. Though there are reduced large-scale battles, skirmishes persist in resource-rich regions. The border remains militarized, with both nations relying on international peacekeepers to maintain fragile ceasefires. Political instability in Khartoum and Juba hampers long-term peace.
