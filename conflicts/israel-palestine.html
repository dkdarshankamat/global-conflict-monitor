Israel – Palestine Conflict (2025–2040)

Estimated Deaths

Between 2025 and 2040, the Israel–Palestine conflict saw an estimated 90,000+ deaths due to continued urban warfare, drone strikes, and intensified border clashes, especially during the 2029 Gaza escalation and the 2036 Jerusalem standoff.

Historical Peace Efforts

  • 2026: The Riyadh Peace Framework was endorsed by the Arab League but rejected by key factions in Gaza and the West Bank.
  • 2030: A joint EU–U.S. mission established a short-lived demilitarized zone in Northern Gaza.
  • 2033: The Two-State Referendum initiative failed following internal divisions and violence in the West Bank.
  • 2038: The Mediterranean Accord attempted to create an economic union zone including Israel and Palestinian territories—partially successful for trade but failed politically.

Energy & Tech Impacts

The prolonged conflict disrupted Eastern Mediterranean gas exploration. Israeli cybersecurity firms became global leaders, while Palestinian startups emerged through diaspora funding. The 2034 cyber-conflict with Iran escalated digital surveillance across the region.

Current Status Quo (2040)

Israel maintains control over key strategic zones, while the West Bank remains fragmented between multiple authorities. Gaza, though rebuilt in parts, remains under heavy blockade. International recognition for a two-state solution is fading, replaced by smaller bilateral tech and security pacts with third-party states.
